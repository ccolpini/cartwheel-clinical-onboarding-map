M3
Gate · Decision Point
Are Pre-Onboarding Steps Complete? 🔴 1 ⚠️ 1
Day before start date · IT access + orientation readiness check
▾
Path A — Steps complete
  • Receives IT credentials — often late, day before start
  • Limited buffer to resolve access issues before day one
  • Attends New Employee Orientation (People Ops + Clinical + IT)
  • Auto-enrolled in Rippling trainings — no action required
Path B — Steps incomplete
  • Start date pushed — TA notifies clinician manually
  • Late notice is disruptive: childcare, transport, life logistics already arranged
  • No proactive warning system flags risk before this moment
  • Some of these clinicians never restart — a second ghost pool
What the data shows at this gate

Half the clinicians who showed up on their start date never reached Cleared for Assignment. They completed interviews, signed an offer, submitted credentials, and still never saw a patient. This is the credentialing window failure — not a sourcing problem.

🔴
Date changes are reactive, not proactive TA manually notifies clinicians of start date changes. No early warning system exists to flag at-risk clinicians before the deadline. By the time a push happens, the clinician has already planned around the original date.
🔴 Critical
⚠️
IT credentials arrive too late to resolve issues Access delivery often happens the day before start, leaving no buffer. Day-one access problems undermine the first impression at the worst possible moment.

Median 14 days to first intake from official start date (IQR 7–30)
▾
What the clinician experiences
  • Marked cleared for assignment — officially ready to see patients
  • First payer enrollment drives scheduling preference
  • First intake appointment with a Cartwheel patient

What this moment means
  • The funnel problem is almost entirely pre-activation
  • Once a clinician is seeing patients, retention becomes near-certain
  • The battle is the credentialing window — not keeping people once they're in
  • Every day of delay = a day Cartwheel cannot bill that clinician's sessions
✓ The retention unlock

stay with Cartwheel

Once a clinician gets here, they almost never leave. The design challenge isn't retention — it's activation. Every project in the roadmap targets the window between signed offer and first billable session.

Supervised vs. independent — an 11-point gap

Supervised clinicians reach clearance at 72% through-funnel vs. 61% for independent clinicians. Most plausible mechanism: supervision provides a mandatory, paid, recurring touchpoint during the credentialing window. This is a discovery hypothesis — not a confirmed cause.

Reading this map
Where the experience breaks

The biggest drops — at credentialing survey and cleared for assignment — are both driven by invisible processes. No progress signals, no proactive reminders, no owned handoffs. Clinicians fall out not because they chose to leave, but because the process gives out on them.

What the data doesn't show

The emotional weight of a delayed start date. The friction of self-navigating state compliance on a generic Google Site. The confusion when the recruiter's description of the role doesn't match what they find when they arrive.

The implication

At 94.7% retention for fully utilized clinicians, the problem has never been keeping people. It's getting them there. The credentialing window is where Cartwheel loses 72 out of every 100 clinicians it successfully recruits.
